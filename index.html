About Michelle Rushano

February 22, 2022

Today I am declaring my interest in running for Madison County School Board Zone 7, Alabam-Forum area. I have been planning for this for about 10 years, but had to wait until the timing was right for me and my family, so I can be committed to this volunteer position!

While I’m not a Madison County native, I have been in the NWA area for the past 15 years. I am originally a Wisconsin resident until my 30th birthday when I relocated to Kansas City, MO to complete my Doctorate in Pharmacy, and have been blessed beyond measure to care for our nation's Veterans and know many here in the county too. I met a new one today,! He is a 96-year-old WWII Veteran, probably one of you’alls grandad! He was at the Madison County Senior Health and Wellness Center, where I reached out most recently to volunteer as well.

While a college student I found my love for volunteering for many community groups, but one of my favorites was teaching preschoolers about common household poisons and Mr. Yuck, as well as, countless hours at several Free Health Clinics, in Milwaukee, Little Rock, and in Kansas City.

My most recent volunteer position has been a CASA (Court Appointed Special Advocate) which is a community volunteer who is the voice for the child when children find themselves placed in foster care. I’ve been a CASA volunteer in both Madison and Washington County for the past 14 years. Sadly we don’t seem to run out of kids that enter foster care.

I am truly an advocate for anyone who needs one. Ask anyone who knows me. While I’ve been a single parent for the past 15 years, it has given me a great deal of strength and much understanding. And as the saying goes, “the busiest people, get the most done”.

My work ethic has always been to have at least one or two solutions to a problem before I ever approach to say there is a problem.

I’ve always been hard-working, and have been pretty active attending school board meetings already both in-person and online, and have had made some good contacts with several of my own kids' teachers and principals the 5 years we’ve been in Madison County.

I’ve got a wish list for our district already, have independently applied for a nutrition grant for the district last fall that hopefully gets allocated to us this coming school year. I’m really quite excited, highly motivated, and have been dreaming and planning for this for years and would be honored to represent all our parents and children.

I’ve worked hard for everything I’ve achieved and have an objective perspective to bring to the board for those difficult decisions to be made. However, one goal is to support our principals and teachers, so we can have better school days and a healthy learning environment for learning for all children. I pride myself on my being a very independent, single mom, strong-minded, with good character, and completely up for the challenge and look forward to being your voice!

I’ve already recruited, my recommendation from Mrs. Enix, one of our own high school students to create a website where I plan to have polling questions as well as keep our previously, non-transparent board, extremely transparent, and hope you will be part of my vision too! I would love to hear your ideas too.

While we, as new candidates, do need a minimum of 20 registered voters in our district, I’ve already reached out to several to request their support, so will continue to meet with each of you and look forward to meeting with anyone else who’d be gracious to support me in supporting my children, your children and your grandchildren in our Madison County School District.

Blessings,
Michelle Rushano
